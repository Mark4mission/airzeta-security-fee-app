## 1. Overview

The **Airzeta Station Security Portal** is your one-stop web application for all aviation cargo security operations at your station. Through this portal, you can:

- Stay informed with security bulletins and policy updates from HQ
- Submit your station's monthly security cost reports
- Manage and report your station's current security threat level
- Access a curated library of important industry and regulatory links
- View real-time global security news

The portal features a modern dark-themed interface designed for comfortable extended use and works on desktops, laptops, and tablets.

---

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 Accessing the Portal

Open your web browser and navigate to the portal URL provided by your HQ administrator or regional manager.

### 2.2 Login & Authentication

1. Enter your registered **email address** and **password** on the login screen.
2. Click **Sign In**.
3. You will be directed to the Home Dashboard.
4. Your station name (branch name) appears in the Welcome Banner, confirming you are logged in to the correct account.

> **First-Time Users**: If you receive a "Pending Approval" message, your account registration has been submitted and is awaiting HQ administrator approval. Contact your HQ liaison for expedited access.

### 2.3 Your Role as a Branch User

As a branch user, you have access to the following modules:

| Module | What You Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Home** | View dashboard, QR code, news feed |
| **Security Policy** | Read the policy, download PDF, print |
| **Security Bulletin** | Read announcements, download attachments |
| **Document Library** | Browse documents, download files, upload documents |
| **Security Fee** | Submit your station's cost reports |
| **Security Level** | Configure your station's threat levels |
| **Important Links** | Browse the curated link library |

> **Note**: The "Settings & Users" menu is only visible to HQ administrators. If you need station configuration changes, contact your administrator.

## 3. Home Dashboard

The Home page is the first screen you see after logging in.

### 3.1 Welcome Banner & Station Info

The banner shows:
- Your **name** (or email)
- Your **station name** (e.g., "Station: HANSF")
- Today's **date** (in long format)

### 3.2 Time Zone Display

Below the banner, three clock displays are shown:
- **UTC** — Coordinated Universal Time
- **KST** — Korea Standard Time
- **Your Local Time** — automatically detected from your browser

This is especially useful for coordinating with HQ and other stations across time zones.

### 3.3 Module Quick-Access Cards

Three large clickable cards provide direct access to the most-used modules:

1. **Security Bulletin Board** — with previews of the 3 latest post titles
2. **Security Fee Management** — for submitting monthly costs
3. **Security Level** — for managing your station's threat level

Click any card to navigate directly to that module.

### 3.4 Security Pledge QR Code

A QR code links to the Airzeta Security Agreement page. You can:
- **Scan** the QR code with your phone camera to open the agreement on your mobile device
- **Click** the "View Pledge" button to open it in an in-page modal window
- **Open in new tab** using the link at the top of the modal

This is convenient for sharing the pledge with on-site personnel during training sessions.

### 3.5 Global Security News Feed

The bottom section displays a live feed of global aviation security news headlines, keeping you informed about developments that may affect your station operations.

## 4. Security Policy

Navigate to **Security Policy** from the left sidebar.

### 4.1 Reading the Policy

The company-wide security policy document is displayed in a formatted layout:
- **Title** — displayed as a large heading with red underline
- **Section headers** (e.g., "1. PURPOSE", "2. SCOPE") — displayed in blue
- **Bullet points** — properly indented
- **Metadata** (Version, Effective Date) — shown in italic

Read the policy thoroughly and periodically check for updates. The policy content is maintained by HQ and may change based on regulatory developments.

### 4.2 Downloading the Official PDF

If HQ has uploaded an official PDF version of the policy:
1. Look for the **"Official Policy Document (PDF)"** section at the top of the page.
2. Click the green **Download** button to save the PDF to your device.
3. Use this PDF for local printing, distribution to staff, or compliance audits.

### 4.3 Printing the Policy

Click the **Print** button (printer icon) in the page header to open your browser's print dialog. This prints the formatted on-screen text version of the policy.

## 5. Security Bulletin Board

Navigate to **Security Bulletin** from the left sidebar.

### 5.1 Viewing Announcements

The bulletin dashboard lists all announcements published by HQ. Each post shows:
- **Title**
- **Author name**
- **Date published**
- Brief content preview

Posts are sorted by date, newest first. Scroll through the list to browse past announcements.

### 5.2 Reading a Post in Detail

Click any post title to open the full post view. The detail page shows:
- Complete post content with formatting (headings, lists, tables, etc.)
- Translated versions (if provided in multiple languages)
- Attached files (if any)

### 5.3 Downloading Attachments

If a post includes file attachments:
1. Scroll to the attachments section at the bottom of the post.
2. Click the **file name** or **download icon** to save the file.
3. Supported formats include PDF, images, Word documents, and more.

## 6. Document Library

Navigate to **Document Library** from the left sidebar.

The Document Library is a centralized repository for Station Security Operation Manuals (SSOP), regulations, guidelines, and reference materials shared across all stations.

### 6.1 Browsing the Document Library

The dashboard displays all uploaded documents in a table format. Each document shows:
- **Pin icon** (yellow pin) — pinned documents appear at the top
- **Title** — with IATA code prefix (e.g., `[ICN] SSOP Manual v3`)
- **Category** — color-coded badge (Regulation, Guideline, Material, General, Other)
- **Uploaded By** — the branch that uploaded the document
- **Date** — upload date
- **Access** — "Admin" (lock icon) or "All" (users icon) indicating download permission
- **Downloads** — total download count

All users can see all documents in the list regardless of download permission settings. Download restrictions only apply when attempting to download files.

### 6.2 Searching & Filtering

- **Search bar**: Type keywords to filter by document title, IATA code, or branch name
- **Category filter**: Use the dropdown to show only documents of a specific category (Regulation, Guideline, Material, General, Other)
- Documents are always sorted with **pinned items first**, then by most recent upload date

### 6.3 Viewing a Document

Click any document row in the table to open its detail page. The detail page shows:
- **Title** with IATA code and category badge
- **Description** — full document description
- **Upload info** — who uploaded it, when, and total download count
- **Attached files** — listed with file names and sizes
- **Download permission badge** — shows whether the file is admin-only or accessible to all branches

### 6.4 Downloading Files

On the document detail page:
1. If you have download access, you will see file buttons with a **download icon**.
2. Click any file to download it to your device.
3. Each download is tracked (your branch name and email are recorded for the admin).

If you see a **"Download Restricted"** message, the document's download permission is set to "Admin Only" and your branch was not the uploader. Contact HQ if you need access.

### 6.5 Uploading a Document

Any authenticated user (admin or branch user) can upload documents:

1. Click the **"Upload Document"** button on the Document Library dashboard.
2. Fill in the required fields:
   - **IATA Code** — your station's 3-letter IATA airport code (auto-filled for branch users)
   - **Category** — select from Regulation, Guideline, Material, General, or Other
   - **Document Title** — a descriptive name for the document
3. Optionally add:
   - **Description** — a brief summary of the document
   - **Download Permission** — choose "Admin Only" or "All Branches"
4. Attach files:
   - Click the **upload area** or **drag and drop** files directly into the dashed box
   - Maximum file size: **100 MB per file**
   - Supported formats: PDF, DOC, DOCX, XLS, XLSX, PPT, PPTX, ZIP, images, and more
5. Click **"Upload Document"** to submit.

A **title preview** is shown below the input fields so you can see how the document will appear in the list: `[IATA] (Category) Title`.

### 6.6 Understanding Permissions

| Permission | Who Can View List | Who Can Download |
|---|---|---|
| **All Branches** | Everyone | All logged-in users |
| **Admin Only** | Everyone | Only admins and the uploading branch |

- The **uploader's branch** always has download access, regardless of the permission setting.
- **Admin users** always have full access to all documents and downloads.
- All users can always **see** the document list and detail page, even if downloads are restricted.

## 7. Security Fee Management

Navigate to **Security Fee** from the left sidebar.

### 6.1 Your Branch Cost View

As a branch user, you see your station's security fee management interface. This includes:
- Your station's cost submission form
- Historical cost data for your branch
- Currency and payment method options

### 6.2 Submitting Monthly Security Costs

1. Select the **month and year** for the submission period.
2. Fill in each cost item with the appropriate amount:
   - Enter the **amount** in the designated currency
   - Select the **payment method** used
   - Add any notes or explanations as needed
3. Review all entries for accuracy.
4. Click **Submit** to send the report to HQ for review.

> **Important**: Double-check all figures before submission. Contact HQ if you need to correct a previously submitted report.

### 6.3 Viewing Cost History

Your station's historical submissions are available in the cost history section. You can:
- View past months' submitted data
- Track changes and trends in security costs
- Compare month-over-month figures

### 6.4 Uploading Attachments

Supporting documents such as invoices, receipts, or contracts can be attached to your cost submissions:
1. Click the **attachment/upload button** on the submission form.
2. Select the file from your device.
3. The file is uploaded and linked to your submission.

## 8. Security Level — Your Station

Navigate to **Security Level** from the left sidebar.

This is your station's threat level management page. You are responsible for keeping your station's security level current and accurate.

### 8.1 Overview

The Security Level page allows you to:
- View and change your station's current threat level
- Name and configure multiple threat levels (typically 3–5)
- Optionally assign colors to levels for visual identification
- Document action guidelines for each level
- Track the history of all level changes

### 8.2 Viewing Your Current Threat Level

At the top of the page, after the header, you'll see the **Current Threat Level** section. It displays:
- A row of **level buttons** — one for each defined security level
- The **currently active level** is highlighted with a colored border, slight scale-up, and glow effect
- The **effective date** shown below the buttons ("Effective since: YYYY-MM-DD")

### 8.3 Changing the Active Security Level

To change your station's current threat level:

1. In the "Current Threat Level" section, click the button for the **new level** you want to activate.
2. The selected button becomes highlighted immediately.
3. The **effective date** automatically updates to today's date (you can manually adjust it using the date picker).
4. A new entry is automatically added to the **Level Change History** recording the change (from → to).
5. Click **Save Configuration** at the bottom to persist the change.

> **Critical**: Always click **Save Configuration** after making changes. Unsaved changes are lost if you navigate away.

### 8.4 Setting the Airport Code for Map Display

If your branch/station name does not match an IATA airport code (e.g., your branch is named "LONSF" for the London office, but the actual airport is "STN" for Stansted), you can set the airport code manually:

1. In the "Current Threat Level" section, find the **Airport (IATA)** field next to the date picker.
2. Start typing the 3-letter IATA airport code (e.g., "STN").
3. A **dropdown** will appear showing matching airports from the database with their city names.
4. Select the correct airport or type the full 3-letter code.
5. A **green city name** appears if the code is recognized (e.g., "London/Stansted").
6. A **yellow "Not in database"** warning appears if the code is not in the system — the map won't show your station until the code is added by HQ.
7. Click **Save Configuration** to persist the airport code.

> **Tip**: If your branch name starts with a valid IATA code (e.g., "HANSF" → "HAN"), the system auto-detects it. You only need to set the airport code if the auto-detection picks the wrong airport.

### 8.5 Configuring Level Names

In the "Security Levels Configuration" section:

1. Each level has a numbered row with a text input field.
2. Type a descriptive name for each level (e.g., "Normal", "Elevated", "High Alert", "Critical").
3. To **add a new level**, click the **+ Add Level** button.
4. To **remove a level**, click the red trash icon at the end of that level's row.

**Color Auto-Detection**: If your level name includes a color word (Green, Blue, Yellow, Orange, Red, or their Korean equivalents), a color is automatically assigned.

> **Tip**: Use clear, descriptive level names that your team can easily understand at a glance.

### 8.6 Optional Color Assignment

Colors are optional and help with visual identification:

1. Click the **"Colors (Optional)"** toggle button in the configuration header.
2. When enabled, a color picker appears next to each level name.
3. Choose a color by clicking the color swatch and selecting from the palette.
4. To remove an assigned color, click the small **X** button next to the color swatch.
5. Levels without explicit colors default to a neutral blue display.

**Auto-detected colors**: If you type a color keyword in the level name (e.g., "Green Alert"), the system automatically assigns the matching color.

### 8.7 Setting Action Guidelines

In the **"Action Guidelines per Level"** section:

1. Each level has a corresponding text area for action descriptions.
2. Write concise instructions describing what actions should be taken at this security level.
3. Example guidelines:
   - **Normal**: "Standard security procedures in effect. Routine screening protocols."
   - **Elevated**: "Enhanced screening and monitoring. Additional random inspections."
   - **High Alert**: "Maximum screening. All cargo manually inspected. Restricted zone access only."

These guidelines serve as a quick reference for your on-ground security personnel.

### 8.8 Viewing Level Change History

The **Level Change History** section is located below the guidelines:

1. Click the section header to **expand or collapse** it (toggle arrow).
2. The badge next to the title shows the **total number** of recorded changes.
3. Each history entry displays:
   - **Date** of the change (YYYY-MM-DD)
   - **From → To** transition (with the target level name colored if it has an assigned color)
   - A colored left border matching the target level
4. The list is scrollable (up to 280px height) and sorted newest-first.
5. A maximum of 50 entries are retained.

### 8.9 Deleting History Entries

If a history entry was created by mistake (e.g., accidentally clicking the wrong level), you can delete it:

1. **Expand** the Level Change History section.
2. Find the entry you want to delete.
3. Click the **trash icon** (🗑) on the right side of the entry.
4. A confirmation dialog appears: **"Delete this history entry?"**
5. Click **OK** to delete, or **Cancel** to keep it.
6. **Save Configuration** to persist the deletion.

> **Warning**: Deleted history entries cannot be recovered after saving. Only delete entries that are genuinely incorrect.

### 8.10 Saving Your Configuration

The **Save Configuration** button is at the bottom right of the page:

- **Red button** = ready to save
- Click to save → button turns **green** with "Saved!" confirmation for 2.5 seconds
- **Gray button** = saving in progress (please wait)

All of the following are saved together in one operation:
- Level names and colors
- Active level selection
- Effective date
- Action guidelines
- Level change history

## 9. Important Links

Navigate to **Important Links** from the left sidebar.

### 9.1 Browsing the Link Library

The Important Links page provides a curated collection of websites and resources relevant to aviation cargo security. Links are organized into **five categories**:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| 📋 **Regulatory & Compliance** | Government and regulatory body websites, compliance resources |
| 🎓 **Training & Education** | Training platforms, certification programs, educational material |
| 🌐 **Industry & News** | Aviation industry news, security updates, trade publications |
| 🔧 **Tools & Resources** | Operational tools, reference databases, utility services |
| 📌 **General** | Other useful links that don't fit the above categories |

Each category is a collapsible section with a count badge showing the number of links.

### 9.2 Using Category Filters

- Click a **category header** to expand or collapse that section.
- All categories start expanded by default.
- The count badge (e.g., "3") shows how many links are in each category.

### 9.3 Opening Links

**Click anywhere on a link card** to open the URL in a new browser tab. Each card displays:
- **Title** — the link name in bright white text
- **English description** — a brief summary
- **Korean description** — shown in bright, easy-to-read text
- **Category badge** — color-coded pill label
- **External link icon** — visual indicator that the link opens in a new tab

You do not need to find a specific button or icon — the entire card is clickable.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I can't download a file from the Document Library.**  
A: Check the "Access" column in the list. If it shows a lock icon with "Admin", the download is restricted to administrators and the uploading branch. You can still view the document details but cannot download the files. Contact HQ if you need access.

**Q: How do I upload a document to the Document Library?**  
A: Click the "Upload Document" button on the Document Library page. Fill in the IATA code, category, title, and attach your file(s). The maximum file size is 100 MB per file. You can drag and drop files into the upload area.

**Q: My station doesn't appear on the Security Level map.**  
A: If your branch name doesn't start with a recognized IATA airport code, you need to manually set it. Go to Security Level, find the "Airport (IATA)" field next to the date picker, and enter your airport's 3-letter IATA code (e.g., STN for London Stansted). Click Save Configuration.

**Q: How do I change my password?**  
A: Contact your HQ administrator to reset your password or use the password reset link on the login page if available.

**Q: I accidentally changed the security level. How do I fix it?**  
A: Click the correct level button again, then delete the incorrect history entry using the trash icon, and click Save Configuration.

**Q: My station name is wrong. How do I fix it?**  
A: Station names are configured by the HQ administrator in Settings. Contact your administrator to update your branch name.

**Q: I can't see the Security Level page.**  
A: Ensure you are logged in. All users (admin and branch) have access to the Security Level page.

**Q: The Important Links page is empty.**  
A: Links are managed by HQ administrators. If the page is empty, no links have been added yet. Contact your administrator.

**Q: Can I add or edit links?**  
A: Only HQ administrators can add, edit, or delete links. If you have a link to suggest, contact your administrator or post it in the Bulletin Board.

**Q: The security news feed isn't loading.**  
A: Check your internet connection. The news feed relies on external data sources and may occasionally be unavailable.

**Q: How do I scan the Security Pledge QR code?**  
A: Open your phone's camera app and point it at the QR code on the Home page. A link will appear — tap it to open the Security Agreement in your mobile browser.

### B. Troubleshooting

| Issue | What to Do |
|---|---|
| Page won't load | Clear browser cache and refresh. Check internet connection. |
| "Pending Approval" message | Your account is awaiting HQ admin approval. Contact your liaison. |
| Security Level won't save | Check internet connection. Try refreshing the page and entering data again. |
| QR code not scanning | Ensure adequate lighting and camera focus. Try the "View Pledge" button instead. |
| Korean text hard to read | Korean descriptions use a brighter text color for visibility. Adjust screen brightness if needed. |
| Date picker not working | Ensure JavaScript is enabled in your browser. Try a different browser. |
| Missing cost submission data | Contact HQ to verify your branch is registered in the Settings. |
